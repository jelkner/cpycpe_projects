Binary Digits

In the binary number system, a bit is a single binary digit that may hold a value of either 0 or 1.

It would be hard to overstate the importance of understanding bits in the context of learning about computer science. In their book, Blown to Bits: Your Life, Liberty, and Happiness After the Digital Explosion, the authors present seven truths about bits in the form of Zen koans. The first of these is:

Koan 1: It's All Just Bits

Indeed everything we store in our computers, from numbers to images, from programs to videos, is stored as a sequence of bits. The rules governing how a given bit pattern is to be interpreted is called a datatype.

This activity is a first exposure to one such datatype, whole numbers in binary.

Bitwise Operators in Python

Python Bitwise Operators *
Operator Description Example
& Bitwise AND operator. Result bit is 1 only if it is 1 in both operands. 13 & 11 is 9 (1101 AND 1011 gives 1001)
| Bitwise OR operator. Result bit is 1 if it is 1 in either operand. 13 | 11 is 15 (1101 OR 1011 gives 1111)
^ Bitwise XOR operator. Result bit is 1 if it is 1 in one of the operands, but not both. 13 ^ 11 is 6 (1101 XOR 1011 gives 0110)
<< Bitwise Left Shift operator. The first operand is the value to be bit shifted, and the second operand is the number of bits it is shifted, with low order bits filled with 0s. 13 << 1 is 26 (1101 left shifted 1 bit gives 11010)
13 << 2 is 52 (1101 left shifted 2 bit gives 110100)
>> Bitwise Right Shift operator. The first operand is the value to be bit shifted, and the second operand is the number of bits it is shifted, with low order bits disgarded. 50 >> 1 is 25 (110010 right shifted 1 bit gives 11001)
50 >> 2 is 12 (110010 right shifted 2 bit gives 1100)
* The bitwise one's complement operator is intentionally not listed here, since it requires understanding how negative integers are stored.
