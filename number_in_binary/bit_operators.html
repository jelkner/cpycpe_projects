Binary Digits

In the binary number system, a bit is a single binary digit that may hold a value of either 0 or 1.

It would be hard to overstate the importance of understanding bits in the context of learning about computer science. In their book, Blown to Bits: Your Life, Liberty, and Happiness After the Digital Explosion, the authors present seven truths about bits in the form of Zen koans. The first of these is:

Koan 1: It's All Just Bits

Indeed everything we store in our computers, from numbers to images, from programs to videos, is stored as a sequence of bits. The rules governing how a given bit pattern is to be interpreted is called a datatype.

This activity is a first exposure to one such datatype, whole numbers in binary.
